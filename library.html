Eco‑Friendly & Sustainable Plant Care Guide 🌍

Living Soil & Humus Healthy soil is a living ecosystem of fungi, bacteria, and micro‑organisms. Use compost, leaf mold, and worm castings instead of synthetic fertilizers. Avoid over‑tilling to protect soil structure and carbon storage. Water‑Smart Gardening Collect rainwater where possible; it’s softer and plant‑friendly. Water deeply but less often to encourage strong roots. Use mulch (straw, leaves, wood chips) to reduce evaporation. Natural Fertilizers Nettle or comfrey tea, banana peel infusions, and worm castings provide nutrients without chemical runoff or pollution. Eggshell powder adds gentle calcium for stronger cell walls. Chemical‑Free Pest Management Focus on prevention: healthy plants resist pests better. Use neem oil, insecticidal soap, and beneficial insects (ladybugs, lacewings). Companion planting (basil, marigold, nasturtium) helps repel pests naturally. French Organic Gardening Principles Paillage (mulching) protects soil and saves water. Purins de plantes (fermented plant extracts) feed and protect plants. Crop rotation and mixed plantings maintain soil health and biodiversity. Climate‑Smart & Zero‑Waste Practices Choose plants adapted to your climate and light conditions. Reuse pots, recycle soil when safe, and compost plant waste. Prefer terracotta, bamboo, or recycled materials over single‑use plastics.

Eco‑Friendly & Sustainable Plant Care Encyclopedia 🌍

Living Soil & Humus Soil is a living ecosystem of fungi, bacteria, protozoa, nematodes, and micro‑arthropods. Healthy humus increases water retention, nutrient cycling, and carbon storage. Use compost, leaf mold, worm castings, and avoid peat. Water Ecology & Conservation Rainwater is ideal: soft, mineral‑balanced, and eco‑friendly. Water deeply but less often to encourage strong roots. Mulch reduces evaporation by up to 70%. Natural Fertilizers • Nettle tea → nitrogen boost • Comfrey tea → potassium for flowering • Banana peel enzyme → potassium + micronutrients • Eggshell powder → calcium • Worm castings → microbial gold Zero‑Chemical Pest Management Neem oil disrupts pest hormones. Ladybugs, lacewings, and parasitic wasps control aphids naturally. Basil repels flies; marigolds deter nematodes. French Organic Gardening Principles Paillage (mulching) protects soil life. Purins de plantes (fermented plant extracts) feed and protect plants. Jardin en carrés (square gardens) maximize biodiversity. Climate‑Smart Gardening Choose plants adapted to your light and humidity. Group plants to create microclimates. Use terracotta for breathability and moisture regulation. Biodiversity & Ecological Balance Polyculture prevents pests. Pollinator‑friendly plants support bees and butterflies. Habitat stacking increases beneficial insect populations.
